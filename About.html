About Me


Lovejeet Gehlot is currently pursuing Masters in Architecture program at University of Michigan. Graduating in May 2020, his works explore architectural overlays with societies, environment and emotional production. His Thesis project focuses on customized discrete assemblies and re-thinking construction process in order to ease the interim assembly process. His design explorations involves understanding of form-finding methods such as 3D Graphic Statics, Thrust Network Analysis and Force Density Method. He also utilizes structural optimization techniques through understanding of energy density method and load path of the structures. Lovejeet is currently a Teaching Assistant for Structures II course under Professor Peter Von Buelow at Taubman college. Prior to that , he was working in Shapiro Design Lab developing indoor tactile maps for the visually impaired. Lovejeet has led various leadership positions including Unit secretary, and DUGC (Department undergraduate committee) member of the department. He was shortlisted consecutively for the Student Award show in the year 2019 and 2020, and was also a runner-up for the State’s best Thesis project in undergrad in 2017. Lovejeet is a licensed architect in India and has interned at Khosla Associates, Bangalore in the year 2016 and Endrestudio, Oakland in the year 2019. In 2018, he also worked as a design lead in EKOH for the upcoming Bangalore terminal mock-up project designed by SOM, NY.
