Site

The beloved hill in the William G. Milliken State Park and Harbor is located at the outh of the Dequindre Greenway as it spills into the Detroit River. The hill located at the heart of Detroit acts as the highest point in Rivertown and acts as a continuation to the pedestrian and bike friendly waterfront. 
If Bell Island is Detroit’s backyard then the Detroit River-front project is the front yard. The Detroit riverfront hill acts as the terminus for the Dequindre cut connects the Forest Park district to the Underground Memorial statue in down-town with one dedicated conituous bikeway.
Adjacent to this bike highway the famous historic district of Eastern Market thrives with the largest farmers market in the US.
Our site, at the corner of Russell and Division, will reinhabit the block where Shed 4 and the abandoned Eastern Market Garage stand today. Our goal as to learn from the success of Detroit’s common green spaces and appropriate those lessons for the Eastern Market. By creating a new urban hill in the heart of Eastern Market’s concrete landscape we hope to reimagine the district’s purpose. Currently the district is experienceing a relocation of it’s food processing plants due to age and new health and safety regulations, which leaves the current building stock in desparate need to adapt.
We envision the Eastern Market core will retain its market history, but the current warehouse and processing plants will be converted into housing for a new pedestrian oriented district. Our proposal includes 126 new housing units, tripling the size of Shed 4’s capacity, introducing an acre of new green space, and a three-story community center for continued education, daycare, gymnasium, and other activities for the community.
